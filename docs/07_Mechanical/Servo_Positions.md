# Servo Positions & Mechanical Limits

## Purpose
This document details the installation positions, reference coordinates, and angular travel limits of the 7 MG995 servos.

## Servo Specifications Table
| Servo ID | Physical Joint | Mounting Location | Orientation | Min Angle | Max Angle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **0** | Neck Yaw | Top center of spine | Shaft facing up | $-90^\circ$ | $+90^\circ$ |
| **1** | Left Shoulder Pitch | Left side upper frame | Shaft facing left | $-45^\circ$ | $+180^\circ$ |
| **2** | Left Elbow Pitch | Left elbow joint hinge | Shaft facing left | $0^\circ$ | $+135^\circ$ |
| **3** | Left Wrist Roll | Left forearm mount | Shaft facing forward | $-90^\circ$ | $+90^\circ$ |
| **4** | Right Shoulder Pitch| Right side upper frame| Shaft facing right | $-45^\circ$ | $+180^\circ$ |
| **5** | Right Elbow Pitch | Right elbow joint hinge| Shaft facing right | $0^\circ$ | $+135^\circ$ |
| **6** | Right Wrist Roll | Right forearm mount | Shaft facing forward | $-90^\circ$ | $+90^\circ$ |

## Alignment and Offsets
During assembly, the servos must be set to their mid-point positions ($90^\circ$) before attaching the horns to align the limbs with the robot's zero-position references.
